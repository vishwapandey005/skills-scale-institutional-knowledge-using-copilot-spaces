# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads ensure quality standards are met throughout the development lifecycle. They define testing strategy, coordinate test execution, and advocate for quality improvements across the team.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and coverage requirements
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Review acceptance criteria for testability
- Triage and prioritize bugs and quality issues
- Collaborate with developers on test automation
- Report on quality metrics and test results
- Ensure testing environments are maintained and accessible

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Reduce time from code complete to release-ready
- Foster a quality-first culture across the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing status and blockers
- PR reviews focusing on testability and edge cases
- Weekly quality reports and metrics dashboards
- Test planning sessions during sprint planning
- Post-release quality retrospectives

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Pair on test automation, review code for testability
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria completeness
- **Project Managers**: Report testing progress and quality risks
- **Release Manager**: Sign off on release readiness from quality perspective
- **Support/Customer Advocate**: Collaborate on customer-reported issues

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers orchestrate the release process from code freeze through production deployment. They coordinate release activities, manage deployment risks, and ensure smooth rollouts.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule release windows
- Coordinate release activities across teams
- Maintain release checklists and runbooks
- Execute or oversee deployments to production
- Monitor post-deployment health and metrics
- Manage rollback procedures when needed
- Maintain release documentation and change logs
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Achieve zero-downtime deployments
- Minimize release-related incidents
- Reduce time from merge to production
- Maintain clear audit trail of releases

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with delivery teams
- Pre-release go/no-go decision meetings
- Deployment status updates during release windows
- Post-deployment summaries and metrics reports
- Release retrospectives to improve process

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinate merge timing and feature flags
- **QA Lead**: Verify test completion and sign-off
- **Project Managers**: Align release timeline with project milestones
- **Technical Architect**: Review deployment architecture and risks
- **Support/Customer Advocate**: Coordinate release communications and support readiness

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects provide technical leadership and ensure architectural consistency across projects. They make key technology decisions, review designs, and guide teams on best practices and standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical standards and architectural patterns
- Review technical designs and major implementation decisions
- Identify and resolve technical dependencies across projects
- Evaluate and recommend technologies and tools
- Ensure scalability, security, and maintainability
- Mentor developers on architecture and design
- Document architectural decisions and rationale

### Goals
- Maintain system consistency and quality
- Reduce technical debt and architectural drift
- Enable teams to make sound technical decisions
- Balance innovation with stability and maintainability

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review sessions for major features
- Technical design documents and decision records
- Office hours for architectural guidance
- Quarterly architecture roadmap updates
- Cross-team technical sync meetings

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide architectural guidance and code review
- **Product Managers**: Advise on technical feasibility and constraints
- **Project Managers**: Surface technical risks and dependencies
- **QA Lead**: Define testability and observability requirements
- **Release Manager**: Review deployment architecture and rollback strategies

---

## Support/Customer Advocate

### Role Summary
Support/Customer Advocates represent the customer voice within project teams. They ensure customer needs are understood, communicate product changes to customers, and bring customer feedback into the development process.

### Responsibilities
- Represent customer perspective during planning and design
- Triage and prioritize customer-reported issues
- Create and maintain customer-facing documentation
- Coordinate customer communications for releases
- Track customer satisfaction and pain points
- Escalate critical customer issues to delivery teams
- Provide input on usability and user experience

### Goals
- Improve customer satisfaction and reduce support volume
- Ensure features meet actual customer needs
- Minimize customer disruption from changes
- Close feedback loop between customers and development

### Typical Communication
- Customer feedback summaries and trend reports
- Weekly sync with Product and Project Managers
- Release communication planning and review
- Customer escalation notifications
- Retrospective input on customer impact

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provide customer insights for prioritization
- **Developers**: Clarify customer use cases and issues
- **QA Lead**: Test from customer perspective and scenarios
- **Release Manager**: Review release notes and communication plan
- **Project Managers**: Escalate customer-impacting issues and timelines
